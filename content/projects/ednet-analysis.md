## Overview

Analyzed TOEIC learning platform log data from the **EdNet** dataset to develop a richer understanding of item difficulty and learner behavior — going beyond accuracy as the sole evaluation signal.

## Motivation

Accuracy-based metrics miss important behavioral signals: response time, answer revision patterns, and sequential decision-making all reflect learner characteristics that accuracy alone cannot capture.

## Approach

- Extracted behavioral features from EdNet log data (response sequences, timing, revision patterns)
- Assessed **item difficulty** at a granular level using process-based signals
- Characterized **learner profiles** by modeling behavioral tendencies across question types
- Proposed a process-oriented evaluation framework applicable at both question and learner levels

Python, pandas, NumPy, Matplotlib
